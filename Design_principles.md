# Design Principles: Anthropic / Claude-Inspired Personal Homepage

AI implementation reference. The Chinese user-facing version lives in `Design_principles_zh.md`.

## 1. Core Direction

Design the homepage as a warm editorial workspace for a thoughtful builder.

The goal is not to copy Anthropic or Claude. The goal is to translate their design logic into a personal site:

- Trust through restraint.
- Collaboration through openness.
- Clarity through hierarchy.
- Warmth through paper-like materials.
- Creativity through a quiet thinking-space atmosphere.

The homepage should feel like a calm desk, a research notebook, a magazine profile, and a lightweight Claude artifact space.

It should not feel like a loud AI startup landing page, a cyberpunk portfolio, a generic project grid, or a beige template without structure.

## 2. Brand Lessons From Anthropic

Anthropic's brand is rooted in human-centered AI safety: reliable, interpretable, controllable systems. Its visual language needs to communicate human values, trust, warmth, and seriousness rather than cold technological spectacle.

Key takeaways:

- Human safety comes first: visual design should reduce anxiety and create confidence.
- Warmth matters: paper, ivory, amber, clay, and beige tones soften technical subject matter.
- Restraint signals credibility: avoid neon AI tropes, dark sci-fi styling, and excessive effects.
- Editorial structure supports long-form thinking and research-oriented content.
- Functional clarity is more important than decoration.

## 3. Geist Brand System Lessons

The supplied research notes describe Geist's Anthropic system as balancing human mission with technical craft.

Important brand elements:

- Simple wordmark: pure typography with a subtle slash detail that hints at code and future orientation.
- Typeface pairing: Commercial Type's Styrene plus Klim's Tiempos, combining technical precision with editorial personality.
- Warm palette: usable across marketing and product UI, with soft beige backgrounds and restrained amber/clay accents.
- Clean typography, soft tones, and modular layout.
- Minimal and precise design strategy.

Three core values:

- Trust.
- Collaboration.
- Clarity.

Translate these into the personal homepage:

- Trust: honest copy, clear evidence of work, no fake metrics, no inflated claims.
- Collaboration: conversational invitations, visible process, clear contact paths.
- Clarity: every section has one purpose, one hierarchy, and one obvious next step.

## 4. Claude Product UI Lessons

Claude's interface feels less like a futuristic machine and more like a quiet creative tool. It combines ideas from an editing room, a notebook, a chat surface, and an artifact workspace.

Key UI traits:

- Warm natural tones: beige, amber, terracotta, warm clay.
- Handmade feel: soft illustrations, slightly imperfect lines, and human traces.
- Editorial typography: serif warmth plus clear UI type.
- Magazine-like rhythm: generous whitespace, slow pacing, readable long-form content.
- Clear content hierarchy: headings, summaries, lists, code blocks, citations, and warnings are distinct but calm.
- Restrained color: orange/warm brown appears only for buttons, status, or small emphasis.
- Minimal chrome: toolbar and sidebar stay quiet so conversation and artifacts remain central.
- Subtle interaction: the spark indicator can be still while listening and gently animate while responding.
- Function over decoration: system notices, risk cues, and citations appear as subtle style changes, not huge banners.

For the personal homepage, borrow the structure, not the literal chat UI.

Good translations:

- A `Current focus` panel.
- A `Working note` module.
- A `Prototype log`.
- A `Reading trace`.
- An `Open thread` or invitation to continue a conversation.
- Artifact-like project cards with context, status, and next question.

Avoid copying:

- Chat bubbles as the main homepage layout.
- Overly literal Claude UI chrome.
- Decorative AI particles or generic robot imagery.

## 5. Why This Design Direction Works

### Trust and Safety

Warm tones, soft surfaces, handmade details, and whitespace help the visitor feel safe and unhurried. For a personal site, this means the visitor should feel invited into the work instead of pressured by a personal-brand funnel.

### Human Collaboration

Anthropic and Claude frame AI as a partner rather than a dominating machine. The homepage should do the same: present the owner as someone to think with, read with, and build with.

### Simplicity and Comprehension

Use hierarchy, spacing, alignment, and typography before visual effects. Complex ideas should become readable through structure.

### Differentiation

Most AI-adjacent sites use futuristic spectacle. A paper-like editorial site feels more personal, more credible, and more memorable.

### Creativity and Reflection

Trust and emotional safety enable creative moments. The interface should create quiet momentum for reading, thinking, and reaching out.

## 6. Visual System

### Color

Use:

- Paper background: ivory, oatmeal, parchment, warm beige.
- Text: near-black brown, not pure black.
- Secondary text: taupe, stone, warm gray.
- Accent: restrained clay, amber, burnt orange.
- Support: muted olive or moss if needed.

Rules:

- Keep orange rare and meaningful.
- Use accent color for primary CTAs, selected states, small labels, and status marks.
- Avoid neon, cold blue tech palettes, glossy gradients, and heavy dark mode.
- Prefer tonal contrast over colorful contrast.

### Typography

Use:

- Serif display for personal statements, essays, and section titles.
- Clean sans-serif for body UI, navigation, cards, and project descriptions.
- Mono for dates, statuses, labels, and system-like metadata.
- Literary readable Chinese typography when rendering Chinese content.

Rules:

- Let typography carry the personality.
- Use only a few font weights.
- Keep body line-height generous.
- Use uppercase mono labels sparingly.

### Texture and Material

Use:

- Subtle paper grain.
- Soft radial warmth.
- Low-opacity hairline borders.
- Gentle paper-like shadows.
- Optional faint structural grid.
- Slightly handmade details where appropriate.

Avoid:

- Heavy glassmorphism.
- Neon glow.
- AI particles.
- Obvious mesh gradients.
- Loud 3D decorations.
- Repetitive decorative icons.

## 7. Layout System

Use magazine-style whitespace and a clear editorial rhythm.

Suggested homepage structure:

- Hero: personal thesis, short supporting copy, one primary CTA, one secondary link.
- Now: current questions, focus, or active experiments.
- Selected Work: 2 to 3 carefully chosen projects with editorial context.
- Writing: essays, notes, observations, fragments, reading traces.
- Collaborate: what kinds of conversations or work are welcome.
- Footer: restrained links, update date, small personal signature.

Rules:

- One main idea per viewport.
- Do not fill empty space just because it is empty.
- Avoid equal-weight project grids.
- Use hierarchy to show what matters most.
- Make every section answer a specific question.

## 8. Component Language

### Navigation

- 3 to 5 links maximum.
- One restrained contact or primary action.
- Sticky is acceptable, but visual weight must stay low.
- No heavy logo animation.

### Hero

The hero should feel like an editorial lead, not a sales headline.

Recommended pieces:

- Small mono eyebrow.
- Strong personal headline.
- Concise supporting paragraph.
- Primary CTA.
- Secondary text link.
- Optional artifact-like side panel.

### Cards and Panels

Cards should feel like paper notes, not SaaS feature blocks.

Rules:

- Low-contrast borders.
- Soft surface tint.
- Very restrained shadows.
- Few cards with strong selection.
- Editorial titles, not generic labels.
- Status/date/type metadata only where useful.

### Artifact-Inspired Modules

Use Claude's artifact logic structurally:

- Current focus.
- Working note.
- Prototype log.
- Reading trace.
- Open thread.
- Project status.
- Next question.

This makes the homepage feel alive and process-oriented.

## 9. Motion and Interaction

Motion should feel like breathing, not performance.

Use:

- Gentle page-load fade and rise.
- Subtle staggered section reveals.
- Soft hover lift or border tint.
- Calm link underline transitions.
- Sticky header opacity change on scroll.
- Optional small spark-like state indicator.

Rules:

- Motion should clarify focus, state, or rhythm.
- Do not animate every element.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Keep interactions calm enough for reading.

## 10. Copywriting Voice

The copy should be warm, specific, precise, and human.

Good copy:

- Names concrete interests and problems.
- Shows judgment rather than hype.
- Invites conversation without becoming salesy.
- Works for technical and non-technical readers.
- Makes the person feel thoughtful and approachable.

Avoid:

- Generic portfolio phrases.
- Empty innovation language.
- Overclaiming.
- Buzzword density.
- Fake urgency.
